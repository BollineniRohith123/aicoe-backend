UC-003

Log a Meal Using AI Photo Analysis

A user logs their nutritional intake by taking a photo of their meal. The app uses a third-party AI API to identify food items and estimate their nutritional content, offering a low-friction alternative to manual calorie counting.

Actors

Registered User

Priority

High

Business Value

Primary USP

Preconditions
  • 1
    User is logged into the app.
  • 2
    User has granted camera permissions to the app.
Main Flow
  • 1
    User navigates to the 'Nutrition' section.
  • 2
    User taps the 'Log Meal' button and selects the 'Take Photo' option.
  • 3
    The phone's camera interface opens. The user takes a clear photo of their meal.
  • 4
    The photo is sent to a third-party AI service (e.g., Google Cloud Vision) for analysis.
  • 5
    The app displays a loading indicator while processing.
  • 6
    The AI returns a list of identified food items (e.g., 'Grilled Chicken Breast', 'Brown Rice', 'Broccoli').
  • 7
    The app presents these items to the user for confirmation. For each item, it displays estimated calories, protein, carbs, and fat.
  • 8
    User can adjust portion sizes (e.g., '1 cup', '150g') or remove incorrect items.
  • 9
    User taps 'Save Meal' to confirm.
  • 10
    The meal and its nutritional data are added to the user's daily nutrition log.
Alternate Flows

AI Fails to Identify

If the AI returns low-confidence results or fails, the app prompts the user: 'Couldn't identify your meal. Try again or log manually?'. The 'log manually' option allows them to search for food items and enter quantities.

Error Handling Manual Entry Fallback

User Uploads from Gallery

From the 'Log Meal' screen, the user can select 'Choose from Gallery' to upload a previously taken photo. The flow then proceeds from step 4 of the main flow.

Photo Upload Gallery Access
Postconditions
  • 1
    A meal is logged in the user's nutrition journal.
  • 2
    The user's daily calorie and macronutrient totals are updated.
  • 3
    The user's progress data is enriched with nutrition information.
Data Persisted Totals Updated
Business Value

Primary Unique Selling Proposition (USP)

This is the primary unique selling proposition (USP) of the app. It directly addresses a major industry trend (AI for personalization) and a key competitor insight (the opportunity to disrupt manual calorie counting apps like MyFitnessPal with a more convenient method). It significantly lowers the barrier to nutrition tracking for beginners.

Key Differentiator Market Disruption User Acquisition Low Friction
